About me

I am assistant professor at the Department of Mathematics the Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam. Previously, I was a postdoc at the Clinical Neurophysiology Group of Michel van Putten at the Technical University (UT) Twente and at the Computational Neuroscience Research Group of Gustavo Deco at Universitat Pompeu Fabra (Barcelona, Spain).

Research

I am interested in the analysis of heamodynamic and extra-cellular electrophysiological brain activity, with particular focus on functional interactions. Detecting functional interactions from non-invasive recordings, such as electroencephalography (EEG) and magnetoencephalography (MEG), is challenging because of the presence of cross-talk. By formalizing the problems in terms of group actions, results from invariant theory and geometry can be used to mitigate the effects of cross-talk. More recently I have become interested in studying non-Gaussian effects and higher-order interactions (i.e. between more than two brain regions) using methods from generalized multivariate statistics and information theory. Besides these topics, I have a general interest in computational modeling of neural dynamics and electrophysiological forward and inverse modeling.
